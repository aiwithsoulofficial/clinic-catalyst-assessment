Who We Are

Not Another Dating App

Proxamate is Australia's first dating app that shows you real compatibility before you match. Not a personality quiz. Not a curated vibe check. Actual birth chart calculations - the same science used by astrologers for centuries - translated into a compatibility percentage you can filter by.

Built for singles who are done wasting Sunday afternoons on coffee dates with someone who was never a match. Built for people who want to know upfront - where you connect and where you'll challenge each other - so every decision is an informed one.

The Problem

Every Other App Starts Wrong

Photo. Bio. Swipe right. Hope for the best.

That's been the formula since 2012. You match on looks, chat for a week, meet for coffee, and discover you have zero chemistry - or worse, great chemistry but zero compatibility. You ghost. They ghost. The cycle repeats. The whole thing feels like a full-time job with terrible pay.

No other dating app gives you this level of insight before you invest your time.

The Flip

Proxamate Leads With Truth

We flip the script entirely. Compatibility comes first - calculated from real birth data, not vibes. You set your minimum compatibility threshold. You only see people who actually match. Then you look at the photo. Then you say hello.

It's not magic. It's not woo. It's the most logical way to date that's somehow never existed until now. You're welcome, Australia.

Logo Rules

Minimum Size

Logo mark alone: 24px minimum. Full wordmark: 120px wide minimum. Below these sizes, use text only.

Clear Space

Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "P" in the wordmark on all sides. Nothing invades this zone.

Backgrounds

Use on Deep Space, Navy Dark, or gradient purple. Light background variant available. Never on mid-tone grey or busy photos.

Photography Style

Dark with Neon

08 - Brand Rules

Do's & Don'ts

Practical guardrails. These aren't suggestions. They're the difference between on-brand and embarrassing.

Visual Rules

Visual Do's

+
Use deep dark backgrounds as the base Deep Space (#0D1117) or Navy Dark (#151B2B) always. Never light, pastel, or neutral grey backgrounds.
+
Let the neons breathe Magenta and purple glow effects need negative space around them. Crowd them and they lose their power.
+
Bebas Neue in all caps only Never mixed case with Bebas. It's a display font - treat it like one. Go large or go home.
+
Keep photography bold and high contrast Neon-lit, dark environments, confident subjects. This isn't a wellness brand - it's a tech app with attitude.
+
Use gradient combinations consistently Magenta to Purple for primary CTAs. Purple to Gold for premium/feature moments. Stick to the palette.

Visual Don'ts

-
No light or white backgrounds Proxamate is a dark brand. A white background version should only ever exist for print legal documents - never for anything the public sees.
-
Don't use the logo on busy photos The logo mark needs breathing room. Never place it directly over a face, texture, or pattern without a dark overlay.
-
Don't dilute the neon palette with pastels Pink is not Electric Magenta. Lilac is not Stellar Purple. Use the exact hex codes or use nothing.
-
No stock photography cliches No couples holding hands in a field. No smiling profiles on white backgrounds. No "authentic laughter" staged shoots.
-
Don't stretch, skew, or recolour the logo No exceptions. No "just this once." No outlines. No drop shadows that aren't the approved neon glow effect.

Copy Rules

Copy Do's

+
Be direct and specific "Filter by minimum compatibility percentage" is better than "find your perfect match." Specific beats vague every time.
+
Name the problem plainly "Swipe-and-ghost cycle" and "Sunday afternoon coffee dates you didn't need" are real. Use specific language that makes people feel seen.
+
Keep the cheeky tone consistent "You're welcome, Australia" hits the right note. Confident without being arrogant. Cheeky without being immature.
+
Write short sentences when making key points Impact is proportional to brevity. "Photo. Bio. Swipe right. Hope for the best." Four sentences. Maximum punch.
+
Reference real compatibility language "Birth chart calculations," "compatibility percentage," "where you'll connect and where you'll challenge each other" - these are the brand's vocabulary.

Copy Don'ts

-
No mystical or spiritual framing This is a tech app. "The universe wants you to meet your person" is not Proxamate copy. "Birth chart data shows you're 88% compatible" is.
-
No vague love language "Find your perfect match," "discover true love," "your soulmate is waiting" - these are every other app's words. We don't use them.
-
Don't be preachy about astrology Never lecture. Never defend. Just state what the app does and let the results speak. People who get it, get it.
-
No corporate speak "Leveraging advanced astrological algorithms to facilitate meaningful connections" is banned. Say it in plain Australian English.
-
Don't understate the differentiation Never write "another great way to meet people." Proxamate is the first app to do this. Own that confidently in every piece of copy.
